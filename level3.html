How to Spot Smishing

Smishing is phishing via SMS. Scammers use text messages to trick you into giving away personal info or clicking malicious links.

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Urgency: "Act now," "Your account will be suspended," or "Urgent bill." Scammers want you to panic.
  • Unknown Numbers: Especially long numbers or international codes you don't recognize.
  • Suspicious Links: URLs that look slightly off (e.g., pay-pals.com instead of paypal.com) or use URL shorteners (like bit.ly).
  • Unexpected Prizes: "You won a gift card!" If you didn't enter, you didn't win.
  • Requests for Info: Legitimate companies will never ask for your password or SSN via text.

What to Do:

  • Don't click links or call the number provided.
  • Verify independently: Go directly to the company's official website or app.
  • Block the number and delete the message.
